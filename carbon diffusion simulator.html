Interpretation-

Fick's second law: dC/dt = D d²C/dx²
Explicit finite difference: C(i,n+1) = C(i,n) + r[C(i+1,n) - 2C(i,n) + C(i-1,n)], where r = D dt / dx².
Analytical solution: (C - C0)/(Cs - C0) = 1 - erf[x/(2√(Dt))] for a semi-infinite solid with fixed surface carbon.
Case growth: at fixed temperature, diffusion distance follows x ∝ √(Dt), so doubling time does not double case depth.
Arrhenius relation: D = D0 exp(-Q/RT), so ln(D) plotted against 1/T gives slope = -Q/R.
Hardness estimate: approximate post-quench HRC = 20 + 50C, capped at 68 HRC.
Illustrative post-quench microstructure zones: local carbon level is mapped qualitatively to expected post-quench structure.

Analytical profiles are based on the semi-infinite solid solution to Fick's Second Law. When the characteristic diffusion distance (√Dt) becomes comparable to the model depth, finite-domain effects influence the numerical solution and deviations between FDM and analytical predictions may occur.
